Liquid Termiticide Barriers

Liquid termiticides are a common and highly effective method for creating a protective barrier around your home's foundation. This barrier prevents subterranean termites from accessing your home from the soil. Our technicians carefully apply the liquid termiticide to the soil surrounding your foundation, creating a continuous zone that termites cannot breach. This method is particularly effective in eliminating existing colonies that are accessing your home from the ground. The termiticide is formulated to be lethal to termites upon contact or ingestion, spreading throughout the colony as termites interact with treated soil or transfer the product to other colony members. The goal is to create a complete and unbroken barrier that repels or kills termites attempting to enter your home. We take great care to ensure that the application is precise and thorough, paying close attention to areas where termites are likely to gain access, such as expansion joints, utility entry points, and cracks in the foundation. Termite Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer an alternative or supplemental approach to termite control, particularly for subterranean termites. This method utilizes bait stations strategically placed around your property where termite activity is suspected or likely to occur. The bait stations contain attractive cellulose material laced with a slow-acting termite toxicant. Worker termites feed on the bait, carry it back to their colony, and share it with other termites, including the queen. This process leads to the gradual decline and eventual elimination of the entire termite colony. Baiting systems are an environmentally friendly option that doesn't require digging or trenching around your foundation in the same way as liquid termiticide applications. They are particularly useful in areas where liquid termiticide applications may be difficult or impractical, such as near wells, cisterns, or sensitive landscaping. Baiting systems also provide ongoing monitoring for termite activity around your property. Our technicians regularly inspect the bait stations, replenishing them as needed with fresh bait and monitoring for signs of termite feeding. This allows us to track the progress of the treatment and ensure that the baiting system is effectively targeting the termite colony. The active ingredient in the bait is designed to disrupt the termites' growth and development, leading to mortality over time. This slow-acting nature is key to its effectiveness, as it allows the bait to be distributed throughout the colony before the termites die. This method can take longer to achieve complete colony elimination compared to liquid termiticides, but it provides an effective and less disruptive alternative for many homeowners.
